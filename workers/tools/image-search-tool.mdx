## Overview

The Image Search tool enables your Utari workers to find images from across the internet based on any topic, subject, or search criteria. This powerful capability allows workers to discover relevant visuals for presentations, documents, marketing materials, and any other content that requires imagery—all through simple conversational requests.

## Using Image Search

### Basic Image Search

<Steps>
  <Step title="Request Image Search">
    Ask your worker to find images on a specific topic:

    ```
        Find images of modern office workspaces
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review Results">
    Your worker will:

    - Execute the image search
    - Present multiple image options
    - Provide image URLs and descriptions
    - Show preview information
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Images">
    Choose which images you want to use:

    ```
        Download images 2, 4, and 7
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download or Reference">
    Images can be downloaded to your workspace or referenced by URL for use in documents and presentations.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Search returns irrelevant images">
    Improve your search by:

    - Being more specific about subject matter
    - Adding style and quality modifiers
    - Specifying what you DON'T want (e.g., "without people")
    - Including context about intended use
    - Using different synonyms or related terms
    - Breaking complex concepts into simpler searches
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Not enough image options returned">
    Try:

    - Broadening your search terms
    - Removing overly specific modifiers
    - Searching for related or similar concepts
    - Using more general category terms
    - Trying alternative phrasings
    - Splitting into multiple separate searches
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Images don't match desired style">
    Be more explicit about:

    - Specific style keywords (minimalist, vintage, modern)
    - Color preferences and palettes
    - Photographic vs. illustrated
    - Professional vs. casual
    - Realistic vs. abstract
    - Specific artistic movements or references
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Wrong orientation or aspect ratio">
    Clearly specify:

    - "Landscape orientation" or "horizontal format"
    - "Portrait orientation" or "vertical format"
    - "Square format" or "1:1 aspect ratio"
    - Specific dimensions if known
    - Platform requirements (e.g., "Instagram Story format")
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Quality not suitable for intended use">
    Request explicitly:

    - "High resolution" or "print quality"
    - "Professional photography" or "commercial grade"
    - "Sharp and detailed" or "high definition"
    - "Suitable for [specific use case]"
    - Minimum resolution requirements
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
